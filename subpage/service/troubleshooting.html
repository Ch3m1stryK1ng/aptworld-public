    Frequently Asked Questions

    Why is my pulley making unusual noise during operation?

    Possible causes:

    • Misalignment between pulley and shaft
    • Worn or damaged belt
    • Loose mounting bolts
    • Bearing failure or insufficient lubrication

    Solution: Check alignment, inspect belt condition, tighten all bolts to specified torque, and ensure proper lubrication. If the issue persists, contact our technical support.

    How do I properly install a taper bushing?

    Installation steps:

    1. Clean the shaft and bushing thoroughly
    2. Position the bushing on the shaft at the desired location
    3. Tighten the bushing screws gradually and evenly in a cross pattern
    4. Apply recommended torque values (refer to product manual)
    5. Verify secure mounting and check for proper alignment

    Note: Never use excessive force. If installation is difficult, check shaft dimensions and bushing size compatibility.

    What causes premature wear on coupling components?

    Common causes:

    • Excessive misalignment beyond specifications
    • Overloading or shock loads
    • Inadequate lubrication
    • Corrosive environment without proper protection
    • Improper installation or maintenance

    Prevention: Ensure proper alignment, operate within rated capacity, follow maintenance schedules, and use appropriate protective coatings for harsh environments.

    How often should I perform maintenance on power transmission components?

    Recommended schedule:

    • Daily: Visual inspection for obvious damage or misalignment
    • Weekly: Check for unusual noise, vibration, or temperature
    • Monthly: Lubrication check and bolt torque verification
    • Quarterly: Comprehensive inspection and alignment check
    • Annually: Complete disassembly, cleaning, and replacement of worn parts

    Note: Adjust frequency based on operating conditions, load intensity, and environmental factors.

    What should I do if my product is overheating?

    Immediate actions:

    1. Stop operation immediately to prevent damage
    2. Allow the component to cool down naturally
    3. Check for overloading or excessive friction
    4. Inspect lubrication levels and quality
    5. Verify proper ventilation and cooling

    Investigation: Check operating conditions, load capacity, alignment, and lubrication. If overheating persists, contact technical support for professional diagnosis.
